What this shows

The unit circle (radius = 1) with a point P at angle θ. The x-coordinate of P equals cos θ and the y-coordinate equals sin θ. Drag the slider to move the point around the circle.

Primary trig ratios

  • sin θ = opposite / hypotenuse = y-coordinate of P
  • cos θ = adjacent / hypotenuse = x-coordinate of P
  • tan θ = sin θ / cos θ = slope of the radius line

Key concepts

  • Blue dashed = sin θ (vertical leg) · Green dashed = cos θ (horizontal leg)
  • Red line = radius (hypotenuse = 1)
  • Red dot = point P on the circle at (cos θ, sin θ)
  • Exact values shown for all special angles (0°, 30°, 45°, 60°, 90°, etc.)
  • CAST rule: All + in Q1, Sin + in Q2, Tan + in Q3, Cos + in Q4

Special angles to explore

  • 0° / 360° — cos=1, sin=0
  • 30° — cos=√3/2, sin=1/2
  • 45° — cos=√2/2, sin=√2/2
  • 60° — cos=1/2, sin=√3/2
  • 90° — cos=0, sin=1, tan undefined
